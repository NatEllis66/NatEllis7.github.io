OASIS ARE BACK!

The highly anticipated reunion of Oasis has finally arrived, reigniting the excitement of fans worldwide. After years of speculation and brotherly feuds between Liam and Noel Gallagher, the legendary Britpop band is back, ready to reclaim their spot at the top of the rock scene. With their anthemic hits like "Wonderwall" and "Don't Look Back in Anger" still resonating with audiences across generations, the return of Oasis promises not only nostalgic moments but also the possibility of new music and unforgettable live performances. Fans everywhere are gearing up for what could be one of the most iconic comebacks in rock history.

You can catch the Manchester band at numerous dates across the UK and Ireland and also across the U.S., and believe us, this something you will not want to miss.

New Releases

Fontaines DC

Fontaines DC have recently released their 4th album 'Romance' and it has taken listeners by storm. Their post-punk vibe, excellent guitar work and provocative lyricism has led many critics to throw an early predicttaion for album of the year. the album showcases a broader sonic range, leaning into grunge, shoegaze, and even alt-pop territory. Tracks like “Starburster” and “Here’s the Thing” reflect a raw emotional depth, mixing panic and desperation with sharp, poetic lyricism. These songs maintain the band's gritty roots while exploring themes of identity and existential struggle. The album also offers more accessible, mainstream sounds compared to their previous work, with tracks like “Favourite” embracing brighter, more melodic alt-pop, albeit still tinged with introspective darkness. This makes 'Romance' their most varied and expansive project to date.

"The ever-evolving five-piece enter a new phase of transformation on their arresting fourth album – their most considered and intricately crafted work yet" - NME, 2024

Wunderhorse

Wunderhorse's Midas, released in late August 2024, is the band's second album and represents a significant evolution from their 2022 debut, Cub. Described as a grunge-infused and emotionally intense record, Midas showcases a blend of raw energy and introspective songwriting. Tracks like “July” and “Rain” lean heavily into grunge, with clear influences from Nirvana, while songs like "Emily" and "Aeroplane" offer softer, more shoegaze-like moments.The album was recorded live, capturing the unfiltered energy of the band fresh off tour. This results in a sense of spontaneity and imperfection, which adds to its raw charm.Overall, Midas has been widely praised for its emotional depth and dynamic sound, marking Wunderhorse as a rising force in indie rock. ​

"Wunderhorse's second album heralds the arrival of a band that could become generational." - Rolling Stone, 2024

Royel Otis

Royel Otis’ latest album 'Pratts & Pain' captures the Australian duo's knack for blending indie-pop sweetness with a touch of moodiness. Inspired by the South London pub where they often wrote their lyrics, the album showcases the band's breezy yet introspective sound. Tracks like "Adored" and "Fried Rice" display their upbeat, jangly guitar riffs, while songs like "Molly" and "Always Always" explore softer, more emotional territories. 'Pratts & Pain' offers catchy hooks, shimmering melodies, and a sense of laid-back energy, evoking comparisons to bands like The Strokes and Tame Impala. Overall, it's a playful, yet emotionally resonant album that shows the duo pushing their sound forward.

"'Pratts & Pain' is full of sugary sweet, woozy deliciousness." - DORK, 2024
